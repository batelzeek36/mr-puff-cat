# Praziquantel sourcing - bulk powder + OTC

Goal: where to actually buy praziquantel for Mr. Puff and the Maine Coon, ranked by quality, price-per-dose, and DIY friction. The existing comprehensive-otc-care.md says "Elanco Tapeworm Dewormer." This is the deeper write-up: every realistic option, what's behind each label, and why "food-safe" is a slightly misleading framing for a regulated drug.

## TL;DR

- **Lazy + fine: Elanco Tapeworm Dewormer 3-pack** at Petco Hilo / Walmart / Amazon. ~$15-20 / 3 tablets (23 mg praziquantel each). One tablet covers a 5-11 lb cat. This is what every vet will hand you.
- **Better for a stray: Jeffers Worm Buster 12-pack**, $39.99. Each tablet has 17 mg praziquantel + 68 mg pyrantel pamoate, so it covers tapeworm + roundworm + hookworm in one dose. ~$3.33/dose. OTC, ships fast.
- **DIY bulk, decent: Smith Creek Prazi Power 100 g** ($94.95) or Aqua Meds Aqua-Prazi 100 g ($129.99). 100% pure praziquantel powder (no fillers), marketed for koi. ~4,300 cat doses per 100 g = under 3 cents/dose. No USP cert.
- **DIY bulk, USP-graded: Sacred Purity 25 g powder** ($139.99). USP grade, third-party tested, COA on request. ~$0.13/dose. Same molecule as the koi powder, just with paperwork.
- **Compounded by a pharmacy: Chewy Rx praziquantel oral suspension or capsules.** Needs a vet Rx. Best path if you want a flavored chew or liquid for an uncatchable cat.
- **Skip:** PetArmor 7-Way (dogs only, do not use on cats), any "Hartz" cat dewormer, China-direct via Alibaba (legal/customs/quality risk).

## OTC tablets and capsules (cats)

These are all single-active or fixed-combo finished products. No measuring, no math, no "is this the right grade." This is what 99% of cat owners should buy.

### Elanco Tapeworm Dewormer (formerly Bayer Tapeworm Tabs)

- **Active:** 23 mg praziquantel per tablet (single active).
- **Dosing:** ≤4 lb = ½ tab; 5-11 lb = 1 tab; >11 lb = 1½ tab.
- **OTC**, FDA-approved (NADA 140-983), labeled for cats.
- **Where to buy + ballpark price:**
  - Amazon: 3-pack ~$15-20.
  - Tractor Supply, Petco Hilo, PetSmart, Walmart: same 3-pack, similar price.
  - Chewy: 3-pack with autoship discounts.
  - Jeffers / Revival Animal Health / PBS Animal Health: same product, sometimes 1-2 packs.
- **Notes:** This is "the" cat tapeworm pill. Side effects in field trials <2% (salivation, soft stool). Tablets are scored for halving. Can be crumbled into wet food but it's bitter, so wrap it in a pill pocket or hide deep in pâté.

### Drontal for Cats (praziquantel + pyrantel pamoate)

- **Active:** 18.2 mg praziquantel + 72.6 mg pyrantel pamoate per tablet.
- **Dosing:** 2-3 lb = ½ tab; 4-8 lb = 1 tab; 9-12 lb = 1½ tab; 13-16 lb = 2 tab.
- **OTC** per FDA (NADA 141-007), but **some retailers gate it as Rx** (Chewy, PetSmart Pharmacy). Walmart, Pet Shed, BudgetPetCare, 1800PetMeds sell it OTC.
- **Where to buy + ballpark price:** $5-12 per single tablet, depending on retailer. The branded Bayer/Elanco version is pricier than generic; Pet Shed and BudgetPetCare ship generic for under $5/tab.
- **Notes:** This is the gold-standard combo for unknown-history strays. One pill kills tape + round + hook in one go. Worth the slightly higher price over plain praziquantel for Mr. Puff specifically since you have no fecal float and no idea what else she's carrying.

### Jeffers Worm Buster (cat formula)

- **Active:** 17 mg praziquantel + 68 mg pyrantel pamoate per tablet, vanilla/yeast flavored.
- **Dosing:** 2-3 lb = ½ tab; 4-8 lb = 1 tab; 9-12 lb = 1½ tab; 13-16 lb = 2 tab.
- **OTC**, ships from Jeffers Pet (jefferspet.com).
- **Price:** 5 tablets = $18.99 ($3.80/dose). 12 tablets = $39.99 ($3.33/dose).
- **Notes:** Drontal-equivalent at lower price. Same combo, slightly less of each active. Flavored = better odds she eats it. This is probably the single best **value** OTC option for a stray you might be deworming repeatedly.

### Praziguard (Guardian Pet Products)

- **Active:** 23 mg USP-grade praziquantel per gelcap, blended with smoked fish.
- **Dosing:** Same as Elanco Tapeworm Dewormer (it's the same drug, same dose, just in a smoked-fish gelcap instead of a hard tablet).
- **OTC**, sold direct from theguardianpets.com.
- **Pack sizes + price:** 3, 10, 20, 50, 100, 200. Base is $13.95 for the smallest pack. Per-cap cost drops sharply at 50+ count.
- **Notes:** This is the clever middle-ground product. It's the same active ingredient as Elanco (USP-grade praziquantel), but the delivery vehicle is smoked-fish gelatin caps that cats will actually eat without resistance. Useful if Mr. Puff is suspicious of pill pockets. The "USP grade" claim on a finished retail pet product is unusual and worth noting; most OTC pet meds don't bother to label active-ingredient grade because it's not required.

### Tapeworm Capsules 30 mg (Advanced Pharmaceutical Solution, sold via DoMyOwn)

- **Active:** 30 mg praziquantel per capsule, flavored powder inside.
- **Dosing:** 1-5 lb = ½ cap; 5-20 lb = 1 cap; >20 lb = 2 cap.
- **OTC.**
- **Price:** ~$30 for 6 caps ($5/dose).
- **Notes:** Higher dose-per-cap than Elanco (good for larger cats and small dogs). Capsule format means you can pull it apart and mix the powder into food without crushing. Currently out of stock on DoMyOwn at last check.

### Compounded oral suspension or capsule (Chewy Pharmacy)

- **Active:** Praziquantel oral suspension at **5 mg/mL in tuna oil** (typical), 30 mL bottle. Or compounded capsules at custom strengths.
- **Dosing:** 5 mg/kg orally = 1 mL/kg of the 5 mg/mL suspension. So a 5 kg (~11 lb) cat gets 5 mL.
- **Rx required** (vet must prescribe; Chewy's pharmacy fills it).
- **Price:** Compounded capsules listed at ~1/10 the price of branded pills, per Chewy's marketing. Suspensions are ~$30-50 for a 30 mL bottle.
- **Notes:** This is the answer if Mr. Puff won't take a pill. Tuna oil suspension is genuinely palatable to most cats. Requires a vet relationship to get the Rx, so it's only useful once you've done a tier-1 mobile vet visit (see no-vet-plan.md).

## Bulk powder routes

This is where the user's "food-safe" question lands. The honest answer: **praziquantel doesn't have a "food grade" because it's a regulated drug, not a food additive.** What you actually want is "USP grade" (the human pharmacopeia standard) or "EP/BP grade" (European/British equivalents). The closest practical analogs are:

1. **Aquaculture/koi-grade powder labeled "100% pure, no fillers"** — same molecule, no USP testing paperwork, but practically clean. This is what most DIY hobbyists use.
2. **Explicitly USP-grade powder from a research-chemical seller** — pharmaceutical-grade purity with COA available, sold under a "not for human consumption" legal disclaimer.
3. **Compounding pharmacy USP API** — highest assurance but pharmacy-channel only (you can't walk in and buy it as a consumer).

### Koi/aquaculture-grade powder ("100% pure, no fillers")

These are sold for koi pond treatment but the underlying powder is just praziquantel. Many serious cat colony caretakers and breeders dose from these because they're cheap by orders of magnitude. None are USP-certified.

**Cost math for cat dosing.** A 23 mg cat dose comes from 0.023 g of powder. So:

- Smith Creek 100 g / $94.95: 100 g ÷ 0.023 g = ~4,348 doses = **$0.022/dose**.
- Aqua Meds 100 g / $129.99: ~4,348 doses = **$0.030/dose**.
- Wholesale Koi 100 g / $102.67: ~4,348 doses = **$0.024/dose**.

For comparison, the cheapest finished tablet is ~$3-5. The bulk powder is **100-200x cheaper per dose**.

**The catch with koi-grade powder:**
1. **No USP certification or COA paperwork.** The vendors say "100% pure" but you have no third-party testing receipt. In practice this is probably fine (it's the cheapest market and competition keeps quality up, plus pharmacy-grade praziquantel is itself cheap to manufacture), but you can't *prove* it.
2. **No excipient profile.** None of these claim added flavoring, lactose, magnesium stearate, etc., but if any is present, the label probably won't tell you. Unlikely to matter for a cat in cat-realistic doses; xylitol (the worst-case excipient) is not used in any of these.
3. **Dosing math is on you.** A 0.1 mg measurement error matters less than for a benzodiazepine, but you do need a 0.001 g resolution scale (~$25 on Amazon). You can't eyeball powder doses for a cat.
4. **Solubility is low.** Praziquantel is poorly water-soluble. If you want to dose by suspension, you need to first dissolve in a small amount of ethanol, DMSO, or vodka, then mix with food. Or just mix the powder directly into wet food (works fine, slightly bitter taste).

**Important:** the cats.com / JustAnswer warnings about "fish-grade praziquantel is unsafe for cats" are technically misleading. The underlying drug is the same. The actual hazard is *dosing math errors* (someone confuses the per-gallon water-treatment dose with an oral mg/kg dose for the cat) and *unverified purity*. If you measure correctly with a real scale and trust the vendor, the koi-grade powder is the same praziquantel that's in the Elanco tablet.

### Compounding pharmacy bulk API (Rx-channel)

- **Suppliers:** PCCA (Professional Compounding Centers of America), Medisca, Spectrum Chemical, Letco Medical.
- **Access:** These suppliers sell **only to licensed compounding pharmacies, not to consumers**. You can't open an account directly.
- **What this means for you:** if you want compounding-pharmacy-tier praziquantel, you need a vet to write you a prescription, then have a compounding pharmacy fill it. Wedgewood Pharmacy, Diamondback Drugs (now merged with Wedgewood), Roadrunner Pharmacy, Stokes Pharmacy, Mixlab, Pace Pharmacy will all fill flavored chews, tuna oil suspensions, transdermal gels, or custom-strength capsules.
- **Cost:** higher than OTC ($30-80 for a flavored chew bottle), but quality assurance is maximum. The compounding pharmacy is licensed, audited, USP <795> compliant.

Useful if you want to combine praziquantel + something else (e.g. with mirtazapine for an inappetent cat, or in a transdermal gel for a cat that won't swallow).

### Chinese bulk-powder direct (Alibaba / Made-in-China)

- **Suppliers:** Miracle Pharmaceutical, Xi'an Sonwu Biotech, Xi'an Chen Lang, Fengchen Group, Jiayi, Dingyanchem.
- **Specs:** All claim USP/EP/BP standards at ≥99% purity, CAS 55268-74-1.
- **Wholesale prices:** Roughly $30-100 per kg in 1-25 kg bulk orders. So in theory, a kg of "USP grade" praziquantel for ~$50 = ~43,000 doses = **$0.001/dose**.

**Don't do this.** Reasons:
1. **No way to verify their COA paperwork.** They'll send you a piece of paper that looks like a COA. It might be real. It might not. There is no recourse.
2. **Customs.** Importing pharmaceutical APIs requires DEA/FDA paperwork that you don't have. Praziquantel isn't a controlled substance, but it is a regulated drug, and bulk imports by individuals get held or seized regularly.
3. **Minimum order quantities** are usually 1 kg+. You don't need 43,000 cat doses.
4. **Quality variance.** Some Chinese API manufacturers are world-class (the global supply chain depends on them). Some are sketchy. Without an industry relationship you can't tell which is which.

This is the route for someone running a TNR colony of 50+ cats with budget pressure, not for one stray.

## Why "food-safe" is a slightly off framing

You asked for "food-safe (or close to it) sources of praziquantel powder." Worth being clear about what that actually means:

- Praziquantel is an FDA-regulated **drug**, not a food additive. There is no "food grade" of praziquantel because it isn't approved for inclusion in food. The grades that exist are USP (US Pharmacopeia), EP (European), BP (British), and informal "100% pure" claims from aquaculture vendors.
- "Food-safe" in the casual sense (i.e. "I'm comfortable putting this in my cat's food") is a function of: (1) purity of the active, (2) absence of harmful excipients (xylitol is the big one to avoid), (3) accurate dosing.
- All the koi-grade "100% pure" powders meet (1) and (2) in practice but don't have paperwork to prove it.
- Sacred Purity, Medisca, PCCA, and Chinese manufacturers all claim USP grade and have a COA. Sacred Purity is the only one a consumer can buy directly without an Rx.
- The Elanco / Drontal / Jeffers tablets are made from USP-grade API by definition (FDA approval requires it). They're the most "food-safe" option even though the framing is a bit of a category error.

## Dosing from bulk powder

If you go the bulk-powder route, the math:

- **Cat dose:** 5 mg/kg orally (label dose). Some sources go up to 10 mg/kg; the Elanco label is effectively ~5 mg/kg for a midweight cat (23 mg ÷ ~5 kg).
- **Margin of safety:** ~40x. Acute toxicity in cats kicks in at 50-100 mg/kg parenteral (ataxia, depression). 200 mg/kg parenteral is fatal. Oral has a wider margin still.
- **Practical dose for a 5-11 lb cat:** 23 mg = 0.023 g.
- **Practical dose for a 12-15 lb cat:** ~35 mg = 0.035 g.

**Tools you need:**
- 0.001 g resolution jewelry scale (Amazon, ~$25). The cheap kitchen scales aren't sensitive enough.
- Small empty gelatin capsules (size 4 or 3, Amazon, ~$10/1000) if you want to capsule-up doses in advance.
- A dedicated weighing dish or weigh paper. Not your cat's food bowl.

**Method:**
1. Tare the scale with the weighing dish.
2. Measure 0.023 g of powder.
3. Either (a) capsule it for later, or (b) mix directly into ~1 tbsp of wet food right now.
4. Praziquantel is bitter. Strong-smelling pâté (Fancy Feast salmon, tuna, anchovy) hides it best. Watch her eat it; cats will sometimes refuse the bowl after one suspicious bite.

**Repeat protocol:** Once now, repeat in 2-3 weeks (catches a second round of tapeworm proglottids that hatched after the first dose), then every 3 months as long as she's flea-positive or hunting.

**Combo with pyrantel:** Pyrantel pamoate liquid (Nemex-2 or generic, sold OTC) is 4.54 mg/mL. Cat dose is 5-10 mg/kg, so a 10 lb (~4.5 kg) cat gets ~5-10 mL. Give pyrantel 30 minutes before or after praziquantel; they don't interact, but separating them helps if she vomits one of them so you don't lose both. Or just buy Jeffers Worm Buster and skip the math.

## Safety, contraindications, the catch

- **No antidote.** If you overdose, you support and wait. The margin of safety is wide enough that this is essentially a non-issue at correctly-measured doses, but it's a reason not to "round up to be safe."
- **Don't use on kittens under 6 weeks.** No safety data, and their hepatic metabolism isn't mature.
- **Don't give parenteral (injectable) praziquantel orally, or vice versa.** They're different formulations. Droncit injectable is concentrated and stings; oral formulations are not designed for injection.
- **Pregnancy:** the single-active praziquantel is considered safe; the combo products (Drontal, Jeffers Worm Buster) have not been thoroughly studied in pregnancy and the labels say to avoid them. If Mr. Puff is somehow pregnant despite the ear tip (rare but TNR mistakes happen), use Elanco Tapeworm Dewormer alone.
- **Don't substitute dog dewormers for cats** unless the label explicitly says cats are OK. PetArmor 7-Way is dog-only; it has the right ingredients but at the wrong ratio and concentration. Sentry Worm-X Plus is similarly dog-only.
- **Drug interactions:** praziquantel has minimal known interactions. Cimetidine (Tagamet) raises serum levels; phenytoin lowers them. Neither is a likely concern for a cat.
- **Renal/hepatic disease:** dose-reduce or skip entirely. The drug is hepatically cleared.
