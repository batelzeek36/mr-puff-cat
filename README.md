## Top 5 priorities given the new info

1. **Vaccinate your Maine Coon for FeLV.** OTC vaccine (Revival Animal Health), DIY subq injection. Highest-stakes intervention now that exposure has happened. Two-shot prime + booster.
2. **Decontaminate the bed.** Hot wash everything, vacuum mattress aggressively, light Adams home spray, mattress encasement. The bed is now a flea-egg reservoir.
3. **Capstar in wet food every Mr. Puff visit.** Stops re-seeding immediately. Buy a 6-pack of CapAction generic ($15) on Amazon today.
4. **Black Flag Yard Concentrate** at Home Depot Hilo. Highest-impact environmental product. Spray everywhere she dens or naps.
5. **OTC FVRCP vaccine for Mr. Puff** when she's calm enough to inject. Same source. Highest-yield single thing you can do for her health.

## What's known vs unknown

**Known:**
- She's spayed (ear tip).
- Hawaii has no rabies (one of the few rabies-free states).
- She has fleas (you're getting bitten).
- Two cats sharing surfaces in your home now.

**Unknown but assumable-positive for planning:**
- FIV status. Treat as positive: minimize Maine Coon's deep-bite exposure. Mostly happens in fights, less likely between two non-aggressive cats.
- FeLV status. Treat as positive: vaccinate Maine Coon, minimize shared bowls.
- Internal parasites. Empirically deworm (pyrantel + praziquantel).
- Dental disease. No DIY fix; flag for the eventual catchable day.

## Carry-overs from the noni repo

The full Big Island pet care guide already lives at `~/Documents/noni/big-island-pet-care-guide.html`. Don't duplicate it. Key opinions worth knowing before you buy:

- **Topical Revolution Plus is preferred over oral Bravecto for cats.** Bravecto and other isoxazolines got an FDA neurological warning in 2018 (rare tremors/seizures). Topical selamectin (Revolution) has the longest safety track record. Bring this up with the vet rather than just accepting whatever they hand out.
- **OTC fallback** if you can't get a Rx same-day: Advantage II Large Cat (imidacloprid + pyriproxyfen, monthly topical) plus Elanco Tapeworm Dewormer (praziquantel) if she's flea-positive. Both at Petco Hilo or Amazon.
- **Diagnostic heuristic:** localized wound = trauma, generalized itching = environmental. Useful if anything new shows up later.
