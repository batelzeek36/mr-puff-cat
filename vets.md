# Vet options for Mr. Puff

The closest vet (Pahoa Animal Hospital) is **not currently establishing new clients** as of early 2026, only maintaining a waitlist. Skip them and call the Keaau clinics first. Keaau is about 15 minutes north of Pahoa on Hwy 130.

## Call list (in order)

### 1. Big Island Pet Care Center - PRIMARY
- 15-1518 28th St, Keaau, HI 96749
- **(808) 966-5402**
- Open since 1994, accepts new patients, aims for same-day where possible
- Reviewed as more affordable than other island vets
- Does FIV/FeLV snap, fecal float, microchip scan, dispenses Revolution Plus / Bravecto

### 2. Kilauea Veterinary Services (Keaau) - BACKUP
- **(808) 966-8582**
- Full pharmacy + lab, microchipping
- Call if BIPCC can't fit you in this week

### 3. The Pet Hospital Hilo
- **(808) 961-0638**
- Confirmed they run heartworm + leukemia + FIV + bloodwork
- Further drive (~30-40 min from Pahoa) but a real fallback

### 4. Hawaii Island Humane Society - Community Veterinary Care (Keaau)
- Lower cost option (FIV/FeLV available)
- Appointment only, slow scheduling, not for urgent stuff
- Worth it if Mr. Puff is otherwise healthy and you just want the snap test + spay confirmation cheaply

### 5. Pahoa Animal Hospital (Pahoa) - WAITLIST ONLY
- 15-2714 Pahoa Village Rd Unit C3
- (808) 930-5888, M-F 8a-5p
- Ask to be put on the new-client waitlist for future visits with Mr. Puff or your other cat, but don't expect them to take this first appointment
- They have a dedicated cat exam room + cat recovery + isolation ward, so worth being on the list long-term

## What to say when you call

> "I've been feeding an ear-tipped stray cat that's been hanging around. She seems friendly and I want to bring her in for a basic workup before I let her keep coming inside, since I have a resident Maine Coon. I'd like a FIV/FeLV snap, fecal float, physical exam, and a microchip scan. I don't know her TNR history past the ear tip."

That gives them everything they need to estimate the visit cost and time.

## Cost expectations (rough, Big Island 2026)

- Exam: ~$60-90
- FIV/FeLV combo snap: ~$40-60
- Fecal float: ~$30-45
- Microchip scan: free (they always have a scanner)
- Revolution Plus 3-pack: ~$60-80
- Total realistic out-the-door: **$200-300** assuming nothing weird shows up

If it's $400+, ask for an itemized estimate before they run anything beyond the snap and physical.

## Logistics

- **Carrier:** if you don't have one, ask the vet to lend you one for the visit, or borrow from a neighbor. Top-loading hard plastic is easiest with a stray. Don't try to drive 15 min with a loose cat.
- **Same day prep:** withhold food 4 hrs before the appointment if they're doing bloodwork. Confirm when you call.
- **Resident cat protection:** keep Mr. Puff out of the bedroom and away from your Maine Coon's food/water/litter until the FIV/FeLV result is back. FIV transmits primarily through deep bite wounds, FeLV through saliva sharing, so even casual sharing of bowls is a concern.
