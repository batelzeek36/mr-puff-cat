Top 5 Priorities

If you do nothing else, do these. In order.

  1. Vaccinate the Maine Coon for FeLV Highest-stakes intervention now that exposure has happened. OTC vaccine from Revival Animal Health, two-shot prime + booster, ~$30 total. DIY subq.
  2. Decontaminate the bed and bedroom Hot wash all bedding, vacuum mattress aggressively, light Adams home spray on bare mattress, $30 mattress encasement. The bed is now a flea-egg reservoir.
  3. Capstar / CapAction in wet food every visit OTC oral pill, kills 100% of fleas in 30 min, lasts 24 hrs. Stops re-seeding immediately. ~$15 generic 6-pack on Amazon.
  4. Black Flag Yard Concentrate at Home Depot Hilo Pyriproxyfen IGR + lambda-cyhalothrin, 32 oz hose-end, 5,000 sqft. Highest-impact environmental product. Re-treat every 4 months.
  5. OTC FVRCP vaccine for Mr. Puff when she's calm Same source as the FeLV vaccine. Single highest-yield thing for her health. Prime + booster in 3-4 weeks + annual.

01The New Reality

paradigm-shift.md

She's been inside for full days twice now and slept on the bed. The original "keep her separated until vet results" line is already broken. Pivot to harm reduction at the surface level, not prevention.

  • She's a part-time house cat, not a stray you're considering helping. Two overnight visits with no panic from her means she's probably going to keep doing it, and the visits will get longer.
  • Your Maine Coon has been exposed. Anything Mr. Puff carries that's transmitted via shared surfaces (FeLV, URI viruses, ringworm, fleas) has had a chance to transfer.
  • The ear tip tells you she was spayed and microchipped during a TNR cycle. It does NOT tell you her FIV/FeLV status. Budget TNR programs in Hawaii usually skip the snap test because positive cats are still released under standard TNR ethics.
Assume status unknown Treat Mr. Puff as if she could be FIV-positive AND FeLV-positive for planning purposes. Vaccinate the Maine Coon, minimize shared bowls, watch for symptoms.

What you CAN'T do at home

  • FIV/FeLV blood test. Requires venipuncture skill. The load-bearing unknown.
  • Bloodwork (CBC, chemistry panel, T4 thyroid). No DIY path.
  • Dental cleaning, extractions, dental X-rays. Requires general anesthesia.
  • Surgery. Spay is already done (ear tip), but anything else needs a clinic.
  • X-rays, ultrasound. Imaging requires equipment.
Boundary You can do all routine preventive care + minor wound care + symptom-based triage at home. You cannot diagnose or treat internal disease without imaging or labs.

03Mr. Puff Care

opportunistic-otc.md

Every visit (food-disguised)

All hidden in a tablespoon of Fancy Feast pate. The smell covers any pill.

  • Elanco Tapeworm Dewormer (praziquantel) 23 mg, one dose. Amazon

Visual screening (no diagnostics, just observation)

  • Eyes: clear vs cloudy. Cloudiness = LFA bite (most likely on Big Island), URI, or aging cataract. Squinting = pain or LFA.
  • Mouth: lift the lip if she'll let you. Healthy gums are pink. Rot smell = dental disease.
  • Coat: part fur on the rump. Flea dirt looks like pepper, smears red on a wet tissue. Round patches with raised borders = ringworm.
  • Weight: lift her weekly, get a sense for whether she's getting lighter. Weight loss is always a flag.
  • Ears: dark crumbly debris = mites. Clean with mineral oil on a cotton ball.
  • Wood's lamp ($15 on Amazon) for ringworm screening. M. canis glows green under UV in ~50% of cases.

04Your Maine Coon

high-priority-now.md

Mr. Puff's exposure to your cat is already done. You can't prevent it retroactively. What you can do is harden your Maine Coon's defenses now.

Verify and DIY-boost vaccines

  • Last FVRCP? If >1 year ago for cat under 7, or >3 years for any age, boost now. OTC FVRCP fine.
  • Has she ever had FeLV vaccine? If no, start the series now. Two-shot prime + booster + annual. Mr. Puff has been on the bed; FeLV transmits via shared surfaces, mutual grooming, and food bowls.

Flea control on the Maine Coon

  • Capstar dose now (immediate knockdown of any fleas she picked up).
  • Then monthly Advantage II topical, or call a mobile vet for one Rx visit.
  • She likely has fleas now even if you can't see them. Treat empirically.

60-90 day symptom watch

SymptomLikely causeAction
Sneezing, eye dischargeURI from Mr. PuffSelf-limiting in vaccinated cats; escalate if she stops eating

Reduce ongoing shared surfaces

  • Block the bedroom door when Mr. Puff is inside. Bed is the highest-stakes shared surface.
  • Two food stations: Maine Coon's food on a high counter, or behind a baby gate she can jump.
  • Two water bowls if you can manage it.
  • Two litter boxes in different rooms if Mr. Puff starts using one inside.

You can't fully separate them now without locking your Maine Coon in a room. Aim for "minimum shared surfaces" instead of "zero."

05Bedroom Decon

flea-egg-reservoir.md

This is now flea-egg territory. Eggs hatch in 2-14 days in Hawaii's climate.

  1. Strip the bed. Hot wash everything: sheets, pillowcases, comforter, mattress pad. If anything is dry-clean only, take it now.
  2. Vacuum the mattress. Both sides if possible. Edges and seams especially. Dump canister outside immediately into a sealed bag.
  3. Light spray of Adams home spray on the bare mattress. Let dry fully (1-2 hours, ventilated). Then put a fresh sheet on.
  4. Mattress encasement (Allerzip or similar, ~$30) is worth it. Zips around the whole mattress, traps any surviving eggs.
  5. Vacuum the bedroom floor daily for 2 weeks, dump canister outside each time. Flea pupae hatch in response to vibration; vacuuming triggers them, then sucks them up.
  6. Wash blankets and throw pillows weekly going forward, hot water, regardless of whether she's been in.

06Yard & Home

environment-first.md

The yard (the source)

  • Black Flag Yard Concentrate 32 oz hose-end at Home Depot Hilo. Pyriproxyfen IGR + lambda-cyhalothrin. Treats 5,000 sqft. Spray any area she dens, naps, or hangs out in. Highest-impact single purchase.
  • Re-treat every 4 months in Hawaii (the label says longer; humidity shortens IGR life).
  • Diatomaceous earth (food-grade) along the foundation perimeter as a non-toxic mechanical barrier.

Lanai / porch / sheltered outdoor

  • Adams Flea & Tick Home Spray (24 oz trigger, fragrance-free) at Walmart Hilo. (s)-methoprene + pyrethrins.
  • Spray cushions, mats, anywhere she sits. Re-treat at 4 months.

Indoor surfaces

  • Vacuum aggressively (every 2-3 days during active treatment). Dump canister outside immediately.
  • Wash any soft thing she touches in hot water weekly.
  • Spray Adams on carpets and furniture. Air for an hour, vacuum, done.
  • Flea trap (light + sticky pad, $10 on Amazon) as a monitoring tool. If you're catching fleas a week after spraying, treatment isn't holding.
Permethrin warning Permethrin is in many dog flea products and household sprays. Lethal to cats, even small exposures. If you treat with anything containing permethrin, both cats need to be out for ~24 hrs and surfaces fully dry. The Adams + Black Flag products listed here use IGRs + pyrethrins (different from permethrin) and are cat-safe once dry.

07For You

human-health.md

Bite and scratch protocol

  • Wash with soap and water immediately for at least 60 seconds.
  • Iodine or chlorhexidine, not alcohol. Alcohol burns; iodine actually kills bacteria.
  • Pasteurella multocida is the bacterium in most cat mouths. Cat bites infect fast (12-24 hrs). Symptoms: spreading redness, warmth, throbbing. Urgent care for amoxicillin/clavulanate.
  • Deep bite near a joint or tendon = ER, same day.

Bartonella henselae (cat scratch fever)

  • Vectored by fleas. Aggressive flea control is the prevention.
  • Symptoms in humans: low fever, swollen lymph node near the scratch, malaise. Usually self-limiting but can persist.
  • Swollen lymph nodes a few weeks after a scratch → doctor, ask about doxycycline.

Ringworm

  • Round, scaly, itchy patch with a raised border (often shaped like a ring) = likely ringworm from her.
  • OTC clotrimazole or miconazole cream (athlete's foot section) twice daily for 2-4 weeks.
  • If it spreads or doesn't respond → doctor for oral antifungals.

Flea-bite relief

  • 1% hydrocortisone cream from Longs Pahoa
  • Zyrtec (cetirizine) non-drowsy in the day
  • Benadryl (diphenhydramine) at night if you want the drowsy effect to actually sleep

General hygiene

  • Wash hands after handling her, before handling food or your face.
  • Don't let her lick your mouth or open wounds. Bartonella also transmits oral-mucosal.
  • If pregnant or immunocompromised, the toxoplasmosis risk from her feces is real — use gloves for any cleanup, or have someone else handle it.

08When to Escalate to a Vet

no-vet-but-emergency.md

Mr. Puff symptoms

  • Off food for >24 hrs (universal "something is wrong" signal in cats)
  • Lethargy, hiding for more than a day
  • Visible deep wound, especially with swelling or smell
  • Cloudy eyes, squinting, pawing at face
  • Foaming at mouth, drooling heavily (cane toad ingestion)
  • Difficulty breathing, open-mouth breathing
  • Stops walking normally

Maine Coon symptoms

  • Same list as above — escalate faster (you have better baseline)
  • Plus: any new lump, persistent sneezing, weight loss
