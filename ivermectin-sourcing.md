# Ivermectin sourcing - tablets only

Goal: identifiable, not-overpriced sources of ivermectin **tablets** (or chewables, which are tablet-form). Cat care plus a deep dive on human stockpile sourcing. No livestock paste, no pour-on, no powder, no horse gel. Two headline findings: **Heartgard for Cats is being phased out**, and the legitimate human-tablet market in 2026 is far better-stocked and cheaper than it was during the COVID-era chaos.

## TL;DR

- **Cat-specific tablet ivermectin is essentially gone from the US market.** Heartgard for Cats (the canonical 55 mcg / 165 mcg chewable) is being discontinued by Boehringer Ingelheim. Topical Revolution Plus (selamectin) is the de facto cat replacement.
- **For dogs (or off-label cat use under a vet): Tri-Heart Plus 6-pack** at PetCareRx or Allivet, ~$28.99 = ~$4.83/dose. Generic Heartgard Plus, Rx required.
- **Cheapest US human Rx, identifiable, FDA-approved: GoodRx coupon at Walmart/Costco/Kroger** — $27.75 / 20 tabs of 3 mg = $1.39/tab. Most likely fills as Edenbridge generic (NDC 42799-806).
- **Best DTC US (no insurance hassle): Cost Plus Drugs** — generic ivermectin 3 mg, online order with Rx, transparent cost-plus pricing (manufactured cost + $5 dispensing + $5 shipping). Honeybee Health is the runner-up at $65 starting.
- **Telehealth + pharmacy combo: Push Health** — ~$60 telehealth visit, prescription emailed, fill at any pharmacy. K Health and Hims similar.
- **Cheapest US OTC (no Rx, no telehealth): live in TN, AR, ID, LA, or TX.** Forest Park Pharmacy (Texas) sells 40 ct 3 mg for $69 ($1.73/tab) under HB 25.
- **No-Rx international: Ivexterm 6 mg 2-pack** from Mexipharmacy, $16.42 USD ($8.21/tab). Bausch Health manufacture, ships to US.
- **Best international generic value: Indian Cipla Ivermectol 12 mg** at Apollo/MedPlus/PharmEasy (~₹83-318/strip = ~$1-4) but US import is the friction.
- **Long-shelf-life household stockpile: Sacred Purity USP 5 mg, 1000 ct for $649.99** ($0.65/pill). Best per-mg deal of any tablet source.
- **Skip:** horse paste (not tablets, fatal-overdose-risk in cats), AllDayChemist (not CIPA-verified), random Aliexpress (counterfeit risk per TGA Australia).

## Veterinary chewable tablets (cats + dogs)

### Heartgard for Cats (discontinuing)

- **Active:** 55 mcg ivermectin per chewable (≤5 lb cats) or 165 mcg (5-15 lb cats).
- **Status:** Being discontinued by Boehringer Ingelheim. VetRxDirect, 1800PetMeds, and most retailers have removed it. Chewy still has stock as of May 2026 but no new shipments expected.

## Human ivermectin tablets - the US market in 2026

Three big things changed since the COVID-era ivermectin chaos:

1. **Generic competition expanded.** As of late 2024, Dr. Reddy's + Senores Pharmaceuticals launched Ivermectin Tablets USP 3 mg in the US (the second generic, FDA-approved, bioequivalent to Stromectol). Edenbridge had been the only generic for years. Padagis and Teva also have FDA-approved ivermectin products in the US market.
2. **WHO prequalification.** Edenbridge ivermectin 3 mg was WHO-prequalified in February 2026 (formal global-quality validation). This is a meaningful quality signal — the same drug that Mectizan-program donations use is now what your local pharmacy stocks.
3. **OTC laws.** Five states (TN, AR, ID, LA, TX) passed laws between 2022-2025 letting pharmacies dispense ivermectin without a prescription. Several more states have pending legislation.

## Counterfeit detection - how to spot fake ivermectin

The Australian Therapeutic Goods Administration (TGA) found imported "ivermectin" tablets that were either underdosed or bacterially contaminated. The COVID frenzy spawned a counterfeit market that hasn't fully cleaned up. Real product checks:

- **NDC traceability.** US-sold pills have an NDC (National Drug Code) printed on the bottle and matching DailyMed and FDA.gov entries. Look up the NDC and verify pill imprint, color, and shape match.
- **Pill imprint database.** Drugs.com pill identifier database covers all FDA-approved imprints. If your pill imprint isn't in the database, it's not US-sourced or it's counterfeit.
- **Manufacturer's name on the strip/bottle.** Tier-1 Indian brands (Cipla, Sun, Dr Reddy's) print the manufacturer prominently on the foil. No-name strips are a red flag.
- **Sealed packaging.** Original-manufacturer packaging is sealed. Tampered seals or "loose-pack" repackaged tablets from random online sellers are a red flag.
- **Pill consistency.** Cracked, bubbled, or crumbly pills indicate moisture damage or improper manufacture. Legitimate cGMP pills look factory-finished.
- **Misspellings.** Counterfeit packaging often has subtle misspellings of "ivermectin," manufacturer names, or "Stromectol." Always check.
- **Visual identification alone is insufficient.** A pill that visually matches a real one is not guaranteed legit if it came from an unverified source. Source-of-origin matters more than appearance.

If you suspect counterfeit: don't take it, contact the pharmacy or your doctor, and report to FDA MedWatch.

## Storage and shelf life

- **Labelled shelf life:** 18-60 months from manufacture, depending on formulation. Most US 3 mg tablets are 24-month dating.
- **Storage conditions:** room temperature, 20-25°C (68-77°F). Cool, dry, away from light.
- **Don't refrigerate.** Cold-cycling reduces tablet integrity over time.
- **Avoid bathrooms.** Humidity is the worst storage condition for ivermectin tablets — moisture absorption causes clumping, chemical degradation, and microbial growth.
- **Stability beyond expiration:** ivermectin is a stable molecule, and informal evidence suggests retained potency for years past the labelled expiration when stored correctly. The FDA only guarantees potency through the labelled date; past that, you're on your own. For stockpile, factor in a 2-3 year refresh cycle if you want manufacturer-guaranteed potency.

## Safety in cats - the critical part

Cats tolerate ivermectin **if** the dose is right. Cats die from ivermectin **fast** if the dose is wrong. Two hazards:

1. **Concentrated formulations** (horse paste, cattle injectable) make math errors fatal. A 0.1 mL slip on 1.87% paste delivers ~1.87 mg — enough to kill a 5 lb cat. Tablets are easier to get right because the unit dose is fixed.
2. **MDR1 mutation** is rare in cats but exists. Affected cats have impaired blood-brain-barrier function and lower toxicity thresholds. No commercial MDR1 test for cats; empirical low-dose introduction is the only safe approach.

### Therapeutic doses

- **Heartworm prevention:** 24 mcg/kg PO monthly. For a 5 kg cat, this is 120 mcg = 0.12 mg.
- **Ear mites or sarcoptic mange (off-label):** 200-400 mcg/kg PO once, repeat in 14 days. For a 5 kg cat, this is 1-2 mg.
- **Toxicity threshold:** signs start above ~220 mcg/kg (100 mcg/lb).
- **LD50:** ~80 mg/kg in healthy cats, much lower in MDR1-affected.
- **Safety margin at therapeutic dose:** ~30x for heartworm prevention. Wide on paper, narrow in practice with concentrated formulations.

### Signs of overdose (within 2-3 hours)

Profound depression / stupor; tremors; ataxia (uncoordinated walking); blindness, dilated unresponsive pupils; excessive salivation; seizures; coma. **No specific antidote** — treatment is supportive (IV fluids, anti-seizure meds, intralipid emulsion).

### Drug interactions

Avoid concurrent benzodiazepines, ketamine, erythromycin, spinosad (Comfortis), or some antifungals (ketoconazole). All compound the neurological depressant effects.

## Dosing from tablets

### Cat (off-label oral)

- **Pick the smallest tablet you can find.** 3 mg US generic is easiest to split. 5 mg Sacred Purity is workable. 6 mg Ivexterm is harder. 12 mg Indian generic is overkill.
- **For a 10 lb (~4.5 kg) cat at 200 mcg/kg:** target 900 mcg = 0.9 mg.
- **From a 3 mg tablet:** ~1/3 of a tablet. Cut with a pill cutter, weigh on a 0.001 g scale.
- **Mix into wet food.** Bitter; Fancy Feast pâté hides it.
- **Don't repeat sooner than 14 days.** Long half-life (~3 days oral); accumulation risk.

### Adult human

- **Strongyloidiasis or scabies:** 200 mcg/kg, single oral dose. A 70 kg adult takes ~14 mg = 4-5 of the 3 mg tablets.
- **Scabies repeat:** some protocols dose at day 1 and day 8.
- **Taken on empty stomach** for label dosing; food can affect absorption.
