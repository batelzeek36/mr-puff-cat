# Ivermectin sourcing - tablets only

Goal: identifiable, not-overpriced sources of ivermectin **tablets** (or chewables, which are tablet-form). Cat care plus household stockpile angles. No livestock paste, no pour-on, no powder, no horse gel. The headline finding that reshapes everything: **Heartgard for Cats is being phased out**.

## TL;DR

- **Cat-specific tablet ivermectin is essentially gone from the US market.** Heartgard for Cats (the canonical 55 mcg / 165 mcg chewable) is being discontinued by Boehringer Ingelheim. VetRxDirect and 1800PetMeds list it as no longer available. Chewy still has stock as of May 2026, but nobody's expecting new manufacturing runs. **Topical Revolution Plus (selamectin) is the de facto replacement for cats** and covers ear mites + heartworm + fleas in one product.
- **For dogs (or off-label cat use under a vet): Tri-Heart Plus 6-pack** at PetCareRx or Allivet, ~$28.99 for small dogs = ~$4.83/month/dose. Ivermectin 68 mcg + pyrantel 57 mg per chewable. Generic Heartgard Plus, same active. Rx required.
- **Human ivermectin tablets, cheapest US Rx route: generic 3 mg via GoodRx** — $27.75 / 20 tabs ($1.39/tab) at Walmart, Costco, Kroger. Stromectol brand is 4-5x more.
- **Human ivermectin tablets, OTC: live in TN, AR, ID, LA, or TX.** Forest Park Pharmacy (Texas) sells 40 ct 3 mg for $69 ($1.73/tab) with no prescription, ships in-state only. Tennessee was first (2022); AR, ID, LA, TX all added in 2025.
- **International OTC: Ivexterm 6 mg 2-pack** from Mexipharmacy, $16.42 USD shipped from Mexico. Per-mg the more expensive route, but legitimate Valeant manufacture, no Rx needed.
- **Direct-consumer USP grade: Sacred Purity 5 mg pills, 100 ct for $99.99** ($1/pill, USP grade with COA, "not for human consumption" disclaimer).
- **Skip:** horse paste (Durvet, Bimectin, Zimecterin) — wrong format, concentration too high for cats, easy to lethally overdose; Heartgard Plus DOG product on a cat (concentration too high); Acarexx (it's Rx and not a tablet, just the only OTC-able cat ivermectin product).

## The cat-specific catch

Mr. Puff is the project's reason for this research. Worth being honest:

- **Cats almost never need oral ivermectin tablets.** The standard cat-relevant uses are: heartworm prevention (now better covered by topical Revolution Plus, which also handles fleas and ear mites in one application), ear mite treatment (Acarexx ear drops are FDA-approved for this and are gentler than oral dosing), and sarcoptic mange (rare in indoor cats, treated topically).
- **Off-label oral ivermectin in cats** is a thing but it's higher-friction than the topicals. Dose is 200-400 mcg/kg PO once, repeated in 14 days. Cats overdose easily. The 30x safety margin at therapeutic doses sounds wide, but a dosing math error with concentrated horse paste is famously fatal.
- **For Mr. Puff specifically, this doc is mostly aspirational.** She'd be better served by Revolution Plus monthly (vet Rx, covers everything she'd realistically encounter). Oral tablet ivermectin would only enter the picture if she developed sarcoptic mange or notoedric mange and you couldn't get a vet appointment.
- **The other use case is human stockpile.** Sacred Purity, Forest Park Pharmacy (TX), GoodRx ivermectin generics, and Mexican Ivexterm are all legitimate household-stockpile options for travelers, parasitic prophylaxis, or "just in case" supply. This doc covers both.

## Veterinary chewable tablets (cats + dogs)

### Heartgard for Cats (ivermectin chewable, discontinued)

- **Active:** 55 mcg ivermectin per chewable (≤5 lb cats) or 165 mcg (5-15 lb cats).
- **Status:** **Being discontinued by Boehringer Ingelheim.** VetRxDirect, 1800PetMeds, and most retailers have removed it. Chewy still has some stock as of May 2026 but no new shipments expected.
- **Rx required.**
- **Use case:** monthly heartworm prevention, plus hookworm control. Beef-flavored chewable, well-tolerated.
- **What to do instead:** Revolution Plus (selamectin + sarolaner) topical, which the existing playbook (`product-notes.md`) already prefers over Bravecto. Covers heartworm + fleas + ear mites + some intestinal worms. ~$15-20/month.

### Tri-Heart Plus / Iverhart Plus / Iverhart Max (dogs)

These are the live, in-stock, OTC-equivalent generics of Heartgard Plus for dogs. Both contain ivermectin + pyrantel pamoate at dog-specific concentrations. **Not labeled for cats.** The dog-targeted concentrations are too high to use on cats safely without a compounding adjustment.

| Product | Active per chewable (small dog dose) | Source | 6-pack price | $/month |
|---------|------|--------|--------------|---------|
| Heartgard Plus (Boehringer) | 68 mcg ivermectin + 57 mg pyrantel | Chewy, 1800PetMeds, vet | ~$72-90 | ~$12-15 |
| Iverhart Plus (Virbac) | same | Chewy, Petco, Allivet | ~$30-40 | ~$5-7 |
| Tri-Heart Plus (Merck) | same | PetCareRx, Allivet, Chewy | $28.99 | $4.83 |

All three require an Rx. All three are scientifically equivalent — same actives, same concentrations, same FDA approval pathway. Tri-Heart Plus is the cheapest by margin and the one budget-conscious dog owners typically buy.

**Off-label cat use of these:** strongly discouraged for two reasons. (1) The 68 mcg ivermectin per chewable is roughly 4x what a small cat would get from Heartgard Cats (55 mcg). (2) Pyrantel pamoate at 57 mg is too low to deworm a cat reliably. Use Heartgard Cats stock while it lasts, or compound, or switch to Revolution Plus.

### Compounded ivermectin chewable / oral liquid (cats and dogs)

- **Suppliers:** SVPmeds, PetScript Pharmacy, Wedgewood Pharmacy, Diamondback Drugs (now merged with Wedgewood), Roadrunner Pharmacy, Mixlab, Pace Pharmacy.
- **Format:** custom-strength chewables or 1 mg/mL oral oil suspension (chicken or tuna oil flavored).
- **Rx required.**
- **Cost:** $30-50 for a 30-60 mL bottle of suspension; capsules in custom strengths run similar.
- **Use case:** if Heartgard Cats becomes truly unavailable and your vet wants to keep a cat on oral ivermectin, compounding is the path forward. Chewy Pharmacy fills these. Also useful for the rare cat with notoedric or sarcoptic mange.

## Human ivermectin tablets - US Rx

- **Generic ivermectin 3 mg tablets** are FDA-approved and made by multiple manufacturers (Edenbridge, Lupin, Aurobindo, others). All are pharmaceutically equivalent.
- **GoodRx coupon prices** (as of May 2026):
  - 20 tablets, 3 mg: $27.75 with coupon at Walmart/Costco/Kroger ($1.39/tab).
  - Average retail without coupon: ~$115.93.
  - SingleCare alternative: $36-48 / 20 tabs.
- **Stromectol** (the Merck brand) costs roughly 4-5x the generic. Not worth it.
- **How it works:** any prescriber can write the Rx (telehealth services like Hims, Push Health, K Health will write it for legitimate parasitic indications). Walk into Walmart/Costco/Kroger, present GoodRx coupon, pay cash.

This is the cheapest **legitimate, identifiable** US source. The pills are FDA-approved generic, made in cGMP facilities, identifiable down to the lot number. There's no quality question.

## Human ivermectin tablets - US OTC (state-by-state)

In 2022-2025, five states passed laws letting pharmacies dispense ivermectin without a prescription. The laws differ in mechanism:

| State | Year | Mechanism | Where to actually buy |
|-------|------|-----------|----------------------|
| Tennessee | 2022 (SB 2188) | Full OTC, no consultation needed | Any TN pharmacy that stocks it |
| Arkansas | March 2025 (Act 369) | Full OTC | Any AR pharmacy that stocks it |
| Idaho | April 2025 (SB 1211) | Full OTC | Any ID pharmacy that stocks it |
| Louisiana | June 2025 (Act 464) | Pharmacist-dispensed under standing order | LA pharmacies; pharmacist confirms eligibility |
| Texas | Aug 2025 (HB 25) | Full OTC | Forest Park Pharmacy ($69 / 40 ct, ships TX or in-store), HEB and Walmart pharmacies stock varies |

**Forest Park Pharmacy (Texas) specifics:** $69 for 40 tablets of 3 mg = $1.73/tab. Ships within Texas only or in-store pickup. Not currently shipping to Hawaii. If you want OTC ivermectin without flying to a OTC state, the international route below is more practical.

## Human ivermectin tablets - international OTC

### Ivexterm (Mexico)

- **Manufacturer:** Bausch Health / Valeant. Made in Mexico. Legitimate, branded product.
- **Strength:** 6 mg per tablet.
- **Pack size:** 2 tablets per box (smallest), larger packs available.
- **Source:** Mexipharmacy.mx — $16.42 USD for 2-tablet box ($8.21/tab). Farmacia del Niño similar pricing.
- **OTC in Mexico** (most over-the-counter "venta libre" pharmacies stock it).
- **Shipping to US:** USPS or SEPOMEX, typically 7-14 days. Personal-import quantities (1-3 boxes) generally pass customs without issue.
- **Per-mg cost is actually higher** than the US Rx generic (6 mg / $8.21 = $1.37/mg vs US generic $1.39/3mg = $0.46/mg). The advantage is not paperwork: no Rx, no telehealth visit.

### Indian generics (Iverheal, Iversafe)

- **Strengths:** 6 mg or 12 mg, sold by strip (10 tablets typical).
- **Manufacturers:** Healing Pharma (Iverheal), Sun Pharma (Iversafe), Cipla, others.
- **Pricing in India:** Iverheal 12 mg / 10 tabs = ~₹750 ($9 USD); Iversafe 12 mg / 10 tabs = ~₹175 ($2 USD). The variance is real — Iversafe is the cheap option.
- **Shipping to US:** sketchier. International Drug Mart and similar online resellers ship to US, but customs holds happen. Quality variance is real even with "Indian generics" — the well-known brands (Sun, Cipla, Lupin) are world-class; the small generics are inconsistent.
- **Use case:** large stockpile, willing to deal with import friction.

### PharmacyChecker-verified Canadian / international

PharmacyChecker.com lists verified pharmacies in Canada, UK, India, and Mexico that ship to the US. Stromectol (brand) and generic ivermectin both available, prices generally between US Rx and Mexican OTC. Worth checking if you want a verified-sourced middle-tier option.

## What to skip

- **Horse paste / livestock paste** (Durvet, Bimectin, Zimecterin, IverEase): wrong format (this is paste, not tablets), wrong concentration (1.87% ivermectin, ~12,000x more concentrated than a cat's monthly Heartgard dose). The famous "horse paste" ivermectin from feed stores is the cheapest source by far ($5-10 a tube), but dosing is exquisitely unforgiving and a math error is fatal in cats. Not worth the savings. **Specifically excluded by the user prompt anyway** ("not powders, just tablets") — paste is the same risk profile.
- **Heartgard Plus for Dogs on a Cat:** wrong concentration. The 68 mcg/chewable in the small-dog version is 4x what a small cat needs and overshoots Mr. Puff's safety margin.
- **Random Aliexpress / made-in-China bulk:** identifiability problems, customs problems, dosing problems, COA verification problems. Same reasoning as the praziquantel doc.
- **Online pharmacies without verifiable physical addresses or licensing:** the 2021-2022 COVID ivermectin frenzy spawned a long tail of dropship operations. Stick to PharmacyChecker-verified, licensed compounding pharmacies, GoodRx-affiliated US pharmacies, or established consumer brands like Mexipharmacy and Sacred Purity.
- **Acarexx (cat ear mite drops):** technically ivermectin, but it's a topical otic suspension (not a tablet), Rx-only, and only for ear mites. Mentioned here only because it shows up in searches for "ivermectin for cats."

## Safety in cats - the critical part

Cats tolerate ivermectin **if** the dose is right. Cats die from ivermectin **fast** if the dose is wrong. The two hazards:

1. **Concentrated formulations** (horse paste, cattle injectable) make math errors fatal. A 0.1 mL slip on a 1.87% paste delivers ~1.87 mg — enough to kill a 5 lb cat. Tablets (this doc) are easier to get right because the unit dose is fixed.
2. **MDR1 mutation** (multi-drug resistance) is rare in cats but exists. Affected cats have impaired blood-brain-barrier function and lower toxicity thresholds. There's no commercial MDR1 test for cats (the dog test doesn't apply); empirical low-dose introduction is the only safe approach.

### Therapeutic doses and toxicity thresholds

- **Heartworm prevention:** 24 mcg/kg PO monthly (Heartgard Cats label). For a 5 kg cat, this is 120 mcg = 0.12 mg.
- **Ear mites or sarcoptic mange (off-label):** 200-400 mcg/kg PO once, repeat in 14 days. For a 5 kg cat, this is 1-2 mg.
- **Demodex (rare in cats):** higher doses, vet-supervised only.
- **Toxicity threshold:** signs start above ~220 mcg/kg (100 mcg/lb).
- **LD50:** ~80 mg/kg in healthy cats, much lower in MDR1-affected.
- **Safety margin at therapeutic dose:** ~30x for heartworm prevention dose. Wide on paper, narrow in practice if you're using a concentrated formulation.

### Signs of overdose in cats

Develop within 2-3 hours:
- Profound depression, stupor.
- Tremors.
- Ataxia (uncoordinated walking).
- Blindness, dilated unresponsive pupils.
- Excessive salivation.
- Seizures.
- Coma.

**There's no specific antidote.** Treatment is supportive: IV fluids, anti-seizure meds, intralipid emulsion therapy if available. This is why "let's just guess the dose" is a bad plan with cat ivermectin.

### Drug interactions to know about

Ivermectin's neurological effects compound with other CNS depressants. Avoid concurrent:
- Benzodiazepines (diazepam, midazolam) — used in some cat sedation.
- Ketamine — common cat anesthetic.
- Erythromycin — antibiotic.
- Spinosad (Comfortis flea pill).
- Some antifungals (ketoconazole).

If you're administering ivermectin and the cat has been on any of these recently, the safety margin shrinks.

## Dosing from tablets

If you're going off-label oral for a cat (e.g. mange that you can't get a vet appointment for):

- **Pick the smallest tablet you can find.** 3 mg (US generic) is the easiest to split. 5 mg (Sacred Purity) is workable but you'll be quartering it. 6 mg (Ivexterm) is harder. 12 mg (Indian generic) is overkill and impractical.
- **For a 10 lb (~4.5 kg) cat at 200 mcg/kg:** target dose is 900 mcg = 0.9 mg.
- **From a 3 mg tablet:** roughly 1/3 of a tablet. Cut with a pill cutter, weigh on a 0.001 g scale to verify (3 mg pill ÷ 3 ≈ 1 mg, close enough to target).
- **From a 5 mg tablet:** roughly 1/5. Less practical to cut, but possible.
- **Mix into wet food.** Ivermectin is bitter; Fancy Feast pâté (the same vehicle the existing playbook recommends for praziquantel) hides it well.
- **Don't repeat sooner than 14 days.** Ivermectin has a long half-life in cats (~3 days oral) and repeated dosing accumulates.

For human stockpile dosing, the standard for strongyloidiasis or scabies is 200 mcg/kg, single oral dose. A 70 kg adult takes ~14 mg, which is roughly 4-5 of the 3 mg tablets, or 2 of the 6 mg, or 3 of the 5 mg.

## What I'd actually do

For Mr. Puff specifically:
1. **Don't bother with oral ivermectin tablets.** She doesn't need heartworm prevention if she's getting Revolution Plus (covers heartworm + fleas + ear mites + sarcoptic mange in one topical). If she develops ear mites, mineral oil cleaning + visual recheck is the first move; Acarexx is the Rx escalation.

For household stockpile:
1. **If you live in a state where ivermectin is OTC** (TX, TN, AR, ID, LA): buy through a local pharmacy. Forest Park in Texas is the cleanest mail-order option for Texas residents. $69 for 40 tabs = enough for several courses for adults.
2. **If you don't:** GoodRx + a 5-minute telehealth Rx (Push Health, Hims) is the cheapest legitimate route. $1.39/tab generic, made in cGMP facilities. Walk into Walmart with the coupon.
3. **If you want a no-Rx legitimate import option:** Ivexterm via Mexipharmacy. Slightly more expensive per mg, but Valeant manufacture and US-shipping is reliable.
4. **If you want long-shelf-life USP-grade stockpile and like pill flexibility:** Sacred Purity 5 mg pills, 100 ct or 1000 ct depending on appetite. The "not for human consumption" framing is a legal CYA, the underlying powder is genuine USP grade. Best per-mg deal at the 1000 ct.
5. **Don't buy horse paste even though it's $5/tube.** Math errors on cats are fatal; on humans, dosing mistakes are merely embarrassing rather than lethal, but the format is a liability.
