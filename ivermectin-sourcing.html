the headline picks ›

  • Skip: horse paste, AllDayChemist, Aliexpress, and SpeakWithAnMD / AFLDS / Ravkoo (history of non-fulfillment, "predatory" per House Coronavirus Subcommittee).

The Cat-Specific Catch

why this doc is mostly aspirational for Mr. Puff ›
  • Cats almost never need oral ivermectin tablets. Heartworm prevention: better covered by topical Revolution Plus. Ear mite treatment: Acarexx ear drops are FDA-approved and gentler than oral. Sarcoptic / notoedric mange: rare in indoor cats, treated topically.
  • Off-label oral ivermectin in cats is a thing, but higher-friction than the topicals. Dose 200-400 mcg/kg PO once, repeat in 14 days. Cats overdose easily.
  • For Mr. Puff specifically, this doc is mostly aspirational. She'd be better served by Revolution Plus monthly. Oral tablet ivermectin would only enter the picture if she developed a mange that you couldn't get a vet appointment for.
  • The other use case is human stockpile, which is what most of this doc covers. Travelers, parasitic prophylaxis, scabies treatment, "just in case" supply.

Human Ivermectin in the US Market 2026

manufacturers, NDCs, GoodRx pricing ›

Three big things changed since the COVID-era ivermectin chaos:

  1. Generic competition expanded. As of late 2024, Dr. Reddy's + Senores Pharmaceuticals launched Ivermectin Tablets USP 3 mg in the US (the second generic). Edenbridge had been the only one for years. Padagis and Teva also have FDA-approved ivermectin in the US market.
  2. WHO prequalification. Edenbridge ivermectin 3 mg was WHO-prequalified in February 2026. The same drug that Mectizan-program donations use is now what your local pharmacy stocks.
  3. OTC laws. Five states (TN, AR, ID, LA, TX) passed laws between 2022-2025 letting pharmacies dispense ivermectin without a prescription.

Influencer-Pitched Pharmacies

Tim Dillon, RSBN, Alex Jones-adjacent ›

A separate market segment exists: telehealth + pharmacy bundles marketed via right-wing podcasts and influencer codes. Tim Dillon, Tucker Carlson-adjacent figures, RSBN, Charlie Kirk, Kari Lake, Laura Loomer, and Naomi Wolf all do ad reads or sponsored pitches for these. Worth knowing what they actually sell, what's legitimate, and what to avoid.

The Wellness Company (twc.health)

Flagship of the segment. Florida-based, founded June 2022. Operates its own licensed compounding pharmacy and telehealth.

  • Pitched by: Tim Dillon (twc.health/timd, code TIMD for ~$30 off + free shipping), Kari Lake, Laura Loomer, Naomi Wolf, plus a long tail of MAGA-adjacent podcasters. Influencer codes typically run 10-15% off.
  • Chief Scientific Officer: Dr. Peter McCullough. The American Board of Internal Medicine revoked both his board certifications in January 2025 for promoting medical misinformation. The most relevant data point about TWC's clinical authority.
  • Product mix:
    • Contagion Emergency Kit: $324.99 — bundles ivermectin + hydroxychloroquine + azithromycin (Z-pak) + oseltamivir (Tamiflu) + budesonide + nebulizer. Members save 15%. Telehealth intake required.
    • Medical Emergency Kit: $299.99 — broader preparedness kit.
    • Ivermectin + Mebendazole: $349.99 — bestselling combo, marketed for "parasite cleanse" and "cancer-adjacent" research positioning.
    • Standalone Ivermectin Rx Collection — separate ivermectin-only products at varying strengths.
  • Process: order online → 2-minute medical intake form after checkout → licensed provider reviews and (usually) prescribes → fulfilled by TWC's pharmacy.
Honest take on TWC The pharmacy itself is licensed and the drug is real. The pricing is substantially higher than mainstream — the $324.99 Contagion Kit covers maybe $30-40 worth of generic drugs at GoodRx-at-Walmart prices. You're paying for the bundling, the political alignment, and the McCullough imprimatur. The kits ship; they're not vaporware.

All Family Pharmacy (allfamilypharmacy.com)

Family-owned licensed US pharmacy in Boca Raton, Florida, 5+ years in business. Cleanest of the influencer-pitched segment from a legitimacy standpoint.

  • Pitched by: RSBN (Right Side Broadcasting Network) with code RSBN10 for 10% off. Also affiliated with various RSBN-adjacent influencers.
  • Product: ivermectin starting at $3 per capsule (some pages list $2). Standalone, not a bundled kit.
  • Process: secure online medical intake form → licensed practitioner telehealth evaluation → prescription if approved → in-house fulfillment.
  • Reviews: mostly positive on Trustpilot. Customers cite easy process, fast shipping, best prices, sales.
If you want politically-aligned without McCullough Legitimate licensed US pharmacy with telehealth-bundled fulfillment. $3/capsule is more than GoodRx-at-Walmart ($1.39/tab) but cheaper than TWC's bundles. Cleanest pick of the influencer-pitched segment.

SpeakWithAnMD / America's Frontline Doctors / Ravkoo Pharmacy

The COVID-era bundled service. Avoid.

  • Pitched by: historically promoted via anti-vaccine and anti-mask Facebook/Reddit groups, AFLDS-affiliated influencers. Stella Immanuel was the highest US ivermectin prescriber via this route.

  • Fulfillment: Ravkoo Pharmacy (Florida-based pharmacy chain), formerly the AFLDS partner.

Counterfeit Detection & Authentication

how to spot fake ivermectin ›
The fake market is real The Australian Therapeutic Goods Administration (TGA) found imported "ivermectin" tablets that were either underdosed or bacterially contaminated. The COVID frenzy spawned a counterfeit market that hasn't fully cleaned up.

Real product checks

  • NDC traceability. US-sold pills have an NDC (National Drug Code) printed on the bottle and matching DailyMed and FDA.gov entries. Look up the NDC and verify pill imprint, color, and shape match.
  • Pill imprint database. Drugs.com pill identifier covers all FDA-approved imprints. If your pill imprint isn't in the database, it's not US-sourced or it's counterfeit.
  • Manufacturer's name on the strip / bottle. Tier-1 Indian brands (Cipla, Sun, Dr Reddy's) print the manufacturer prominently on the foil. No-name strips are a red flag.
  • Sealed packaging. Original-manufacturer packaging is sealed. Tampered seals or "loose-pack" repackaged tablets from random online sellers are a red flag.
  • Pill consistency. Cracked, bubbled, or crumbly pills indicate moisture damage or improper manufacture. Legitimate cGMP pills look factory-finished.
  • Misspellings. Counterfeit packaging often has subtle misspellings of "ivermectin," manufacturer names, or "Stromectol." Always check.
  • Visual identification alone is insufficient. A pill that visually matches a real one is not guaranteed legit if it came from an unverified source. Source-of-origin matters more than appearance.

Authentication by route

  • US pharmacy fill: NDC on the bottle matches DailyMed lookup; pill imprint matches Drugs.com pill identifier.
  • Mexipharmacy Ivexterm: Bausch Health name on packaging, no misspellings, sealed blister.
  • Indian Cipla Ivermectol: Cipla logo on foil, manufacturer printed prominently, sealed.
  • Sacred Purity: COA available on request via email; ingredient list matches "ivermectin USP + microcrystalline cellulose only."

If you suspect counterfeit: don't take it, contact the pharmacy or your doctor, and report to FDA MedWatch.

Storage & Shelf Life

stockpile considerations ›
  • Labelled shelf life: 18-60 months from manufacture, depending on formulation. Most US 3 mg tablets are 24-month dating.
  • Storage conditions: room temperature, 20-25°C (68-77°F). Cool, dry, away from light.
  • Don't refrigerate. Cold-cycling reduces tablet integrity over time.
  • Avoid bathrooms. Humidity is the worst storage condition for ivermectin tablets — moisture absorption causes clumping, chemical degradation, and microbial growth.
  • Stability beyond expiration: ivermectin is a stable molecule. Informal evidence suggests retained potency for years past the labelled expiration when stored correctly. The FDA only guarantees potency through the labelled date; past that, you're on your own.
  • Stockpile refresh cycle: 2-3 years for manufacturer-guaranteed potency. Longer if you accept some degradation risk.

What to Skip

do not buy these ›
Horse paste / livestock paste Durvet, Bimectin, Zimecterin, IverEase: wrong format, ~12,000x more concentrated than a cat dose. Math errors fatal in cats. Excluded by the user prompt anyway ("not powders, just tablets") — paste is the same risk profile. For humans, dosing mistakes are merely embarrassing rather than lethal, but the format is a liability.
  • Heartgard Plus DOG product on a cat: wrong concentration. The 68 mcg/chewable is 4x what a small cat needs.
  • Random Aliexpress / made-in-China bulk: identifiability problems, customs problems, dosing problems, COA verification problems.
  • AllDayChemist if you can avoid it: not CIPA-verified, not LegitScript-approved, customs holds reported. 20-year operator with mixed reviews — functional for some buyers but no quality oversight. Last-resort only.
  • Online pharmacies without verifiable physical addresses or licensing. The 2021-2022 COVID ivermectin frenzy spawned a long tail of dropship operations. Stick to PharmacyChecker-verified, GoodRx-affiliated US pharmacies, Cost Plus Drugs, Honeybee Health, Mexipharmacy, or Sacred Purity.
  • Acarexx (cat ear mite drops): technically ivermectin, but topical, Rx-only, only for ear mites. Not a tablet.

cats die fast from wrong doses ›
The two hazards
  1. Concentrated formulations (horse paste, cattle injectable) make math errors fatal. A 0.1 mL slip on 1.87% paste delivers ~1.87 mg — enough to kill a 5 lb cat. Tablets (this doc) are easier to get right because the unit dose is fixed.
  2. MDR1 mutation (multi-drug resistance) is rare in cats but exists. Affected cats have impaired blood-brain-barrier function and lower toxicity thresholds. There's no commercial MDR1 test for cats; empirical low-dose introduction is the only safe approach.

Therapeutic doses and toxicity thresholds

  • Heartworm prevention: 24 mcg/kg PO monthly (Heartgard Cats label). For a 5 kg cat, this is 120 mcg = 0.12 mg.
  • Ear mites or sarcoptic mange (off-label): 200-400 mcg/kg PO once, repeat in 14 days. For a 5 kg cat, this is 1-2 mg.
  • Toxicity threshold: signs start above ~220 mcg/kg (100 mcg/lb).
  • LD50: ~80 mg/kg in healthy cats, much lower in MDR1-affected.
  • Safety margin at therapeutic dose: ~30x for heartworm prevention. Wide on paper, narrow in practice with concentrated formulations.

Signs of overdose in cats (within 2-3 hours)

  • Profound depression, stupor
  • Tremors
  • Ataxia (uncoordinated walking)
  • Blindness, dilated unresponsive pupils
  • Excessive salivation
  • Seizures, coma
No specific antidote Treatment is supportive: IV fluids, anti-seizure meds, intralipid emulsion therapy if available. This is why "let's just guess the dose" is a bad plan with cat ivermectin.

Drug interactions

Avoid concurrent benzodiazepines (diazepam, midazolam), ketamine, erythromycin, spinosad (Comfortis flea pill), or some antifungals (ketoconazole). All compound the neurological depressant effects.

Dosing from Tablets

cat off-label + adult human ›

For a cat (off-label oral, e.g. mange, no vet)

  • Pick the smallest tablet you can find. 3 mg US generic is easiest to split. 5 mg Sacred Purity is workable. 6 mg Ivexterm is harder. 12 mg Indian generic is overkill.
  • For a 10 lb (~4.5 kg) cat at 200 mcg/kg: target dose is 900 mcg = 0.9 mg.
  • From a 3 mg tablet: roughly 1/3 of a tablet. Cut with a pill cutter, weigh on a 0.001 g scale to verify.
  • Mix into wet food. Bitter; Fancy Feast pâté hides it.
  • Don't repeat sooner than 14 days. Long half-life (~3 days oral); accumulation risk.

For an adult human

  • Strongyloidiasis or scabies: 200 mcg/kg, single oral dose. A 70 kg adult takes ~14 mg = 4-5 of the 3 mg tablets, or 2 of the 6 mg, or 3 of the 5 mg.
  • Scabies repeat: some protocols dose at day 1 and day 8.
  • Empty stomach for label dosing; food affects absorption.
