The Cat-Specific Catch

why this doc is mostly aspirational for Mr. Puff ›

Mr. Puff is the project's reason for this research. Worth being honest:

  • Cats almost never need oral ivermectin tablets. The standard cat-relevant uses are: heartworm prevention (now better covered by topical Revolution Plus, which also handles fleas and ear mites in one application), ear mite treatment (Acarexx ear drops are FDA-approved for this and gentler than oral dosing), and sarcoptic / notoedric mange (rare in indoor cats, treated topically).
  • Off-label oral ivermectin in cats is a thing, but higher-friction than the topicals. Dose 200-400 mcg/kg PO once, repeat in 14 days. Cats overdose easily. The 30x safety margin sounds wide but a dosing math error with concentrated horse paste is famously fatal.
  • For Mr. Puff specifically, this doc is mostly aspirational. She'd be better served by Revolution Plus monthly (vet Rx, covers everything she'd realistically encounter). Oral tablet ivermectin would only enter the picture if she developed a mange that you couldn't get a vet appointment for.
  • The other use case is human stockpile. Sacred Purity, Forest Park Pharmacy, GoodRx generics, and Mexican Ivexterm are all legitimate household-stockpile options for travelers, parasitic prophylaxis, or "just in case" supply.

Veterinary Chewable Tablets

Heartgard, Tri-Heart, Iverhart, compounded ›

Heartgard for Cats (discontinuing)

  • Active: 55 mcg ivermectin per chewable (≤5 lb cats) or 165 mcg (5-15 lb cats).
  • Status: Being discontinued by Boehringer Ingelheim. VetRxDirect, 1800PetMeds, and most retailers have removed it. Chewy still has some stock as of May 2026 but no new shipments expected.
  • Rx required.
  • What to do instead: Revolution Plus (selamectin + sarolaner) topical, which the existing playbook (product-notes) already prefers over Bravecto. Covers heartworm + fleas + ear mites + some intestinal worms. ~$15-20/month.

Tri-Heart Plus / Iverhart Plus / Heartgard Plus (dogs)

The live, in-stock generics of Heartgard Plus for dogs. All three contain ivermectin + pyrantel pamoate at dog-specific concentrations. Not labeled for cats. The dog-targeted concentrations are too high to use on cats safely.

Human Ivermectin Tablets - US Rx

cheapest legitimate route ›
  • Generic ivermectin 3 mg tablets are FDA-approved and made by multiple manufacturers (Edenbridge, Lupin, Aurobindo, others). All pharmaceutically equivalent.
  • GoodRx coupon prices (May 2026):
    • 20 tablets, 3 mg: $27.75 with coupon at Walmart/Costco/Kroger ($1.39/tab).
    • Average retail without coupon: ~$115.93.
    • SingleCare alternative: $36-48 / 20 tabs.
  • Stromectol (the Merck brand): 4-5x the generic price. Not worth it.
  • How it works: any prescriber can write the Rx. Telehealth services like Hims, Push Health, K Health will write it for legitimate parasitic indications. Walk into Walmart/Costco/Kroger with the GoodRx coupon, pay cash.

International OTC

Mexico, India, PharmacyChecker ›

Ivexterm (Mexico)

  • Manufacturer: Bausch Health / Valeant. Made in Mexico. Legitimate, branded product.
  • Strength: 6 mg per tablet.
  • Pack size: 2 tablets per box (smallest), larger packs available.
  • Source: Mexipharmacy.mx — $16.42 USD for 2-tablet box ($8.21/tab). Farmacia del Niño similar pricing.
  • OTC in Mexico (most "venta libre" pharmacies stock it).
  • Shipping to US: USPS or SEPOMEX, typically 7-14 days. Personal-import quantities (1-3 boxes) generally pass customs without issue.
  • Per-mg cost is actually higher than the US Rx generic. The advantage is no Rx, no telehealth visit.

Indian generics (Iverheal, Iversafe)

  • Strengths: 6 mg or 12 mg, sold by strip (10 tablets typical).
  • Manufacturers: Healing Pharma (Iverheal), Sun Pharma (Iversafe), Cipla, others.
  • Pricing in India: Iverheal 12 mg / 10 tabs = ~₹750 ($9 USD); Iversafe 12 mg / 10 tabs = ~₹175 ($2 USD). Iversafe is the cheap option.
  • Shipping to US: sketchier. International Drug Mart and similar online resellers ship to US, but customs holds happen. Quality variance is real even with "Indian generics" — well-known brands (Sun, Cipla, Lupin) are world-class; small generics inconsistent.
  • Use case: large stockpile, willing to deal with import friction.

PharmacyChecker-verified

PharmacyChecker.com lists verified pharmacies in Canada, UK, India, and Mexico that ship to the US. Stromectol (brand) and generic ivermectin both available, prices generally between US Rx and Mexican OTC. Worth checking if you want a verified-sourced middle-tier option.

What to Skip

do not buy these ›
Horse paste / livestock paste Durvet, Bimectin, Zimecterin, IverEase: wrong format (paste, not tablets), wrong concentration (1.87% ivermectin, ~12,000x more concentrated than a cat's monthly Heartgard dose). The famous "horse paste" from feed stores is the cheapest source by far ($5-10 a tube), but dosing is exquisitely unforgiving and a math error is fatal in cats. Specifically excluded by the user prompt anyway ("not powders, just tablets") — paste is the same risk profile.
  • Heartgard Plus for Dogs on a Cat: wrong concentration. The 68 mcg/chewable in the small-dog version is 4x what a small cat needs and overshoots Mr. Puff's safety margin.
  • Random Aliexpress / made-in-China bulk: identifiability problems, customs problems, dosing problems, COA verification problems. Same reasoning as the praziquantel doc.
  • Online pharmacies without verifiable physical addresses or licensing: the 2021-2022 COVID ivermectin frenzy spawned a long tail of dropship operations. Stick to PharmacyChecker-verified, licensed compounding pharmacies, GoodRx-affiliated US pharmacies, or established consumer brands like Mexipharmacy and Sacred Purity.
  • Acarexx (cat ear mite drops): technically ivermectin, but it's a topical otic suspension (not a tablet), Rx-only, and only for ear mites. Mentioned here only because it shows up in searches for "ivermectin for cats."

Safety in Cats - The Critical Part

cats die fast from wrong doses ›
The two hazards
  1. Concentrated formulations (horse paste, cattle injectable) make math errors fatal. A 0.1 mL slip on 1.87% paste delivers ~1.87 mg — enough to kill a 5 lb cat. Tablets (this doc) are easier to get right because the unit dose is fixed.
  2. MDR1 mutation (multi-drug resistance) is rare in cats but exists. Affected cats have impaired blood-brain-barrier function and lower toxicity thresholds. There's no commercial MDR1 test for cats; empirical low-dose introduction is the only safe approach.

Therapeutic doses and toxicity thresholds

  • Heartworm prevention: 24 mcg/kg PO monthly (Heartgard Cats label). For a 5 kg cat, this is 120 mcg = 0.12 mg.
  • Ear mites or sarcoptic mange (off-label): 200-400 mcg/kg PO once, repeat in 14 days. For a 5 kg cat, this is 1-2 mg.
  • Demodex (rare in cats): higher doses, vet-supervised only.
  • Toxicity threshold: signs start above ~220 mcg/kg (100 mcg/lb).
  • LD50: ~80 mg/kg in healthy cats, much lower in MDR1-affected.
  • Safety margin at therapeutic dose: ~30x for heartworm prevention. Wide on paper, narrow in practice if using a concentrated formulation.

Signs of overdose in cats (within 2-3 hours)

  • Profound depression, stupor
  • Tremors
  • Ataxia (uncoordinated walking)
  • Blindness, dilated unresponsive pupils
  • Excessive salivation
  • Seizures
  • Coma
No specific antidote Treatment is supportive: IV fluids, anti-seizure meds, intralipid emulsion therapy if available. This is why "let's just guess the dose" is a bad plan with cat ivermectin.

Drug interactions

Ivermectin's neurological effects compound with other CNS depressants. Avoid concurrent:

  • Benzodiazepines (diazepam, midazolam) — used in some cat sedation.
  • Ketamine — common cat anesthetic.
  • Erythromycin — antibiotic.
  • Spinosad (Comfortis flea pill).
  • Some antifungals (ketoconazole).

If the cat has been on any of these recently, the safety margin shrinks.

Dosing from Tablets

the math, off-label cat use ›

For a cat (off-label oral, e.g. mange, no vet)

  • Pick the smallest tablet you can find. 3 mg (US generic) is the easiest to split. 5 mg (Sacred Purity) is workable but you'll be quartering it. 6 mg (Ivexterm) is harder. 12 mg (Indian generic) is overkill and impractical.
  • For a 10 lb (~4.5 kg) cat at 200 mcg/kg: target dose is 900 mcg = 0.9 mg.
  • From a 3 mg tablet: roughly 1/3 of a tablet. Cut with a pill cutter, weigh on a 0.001 g scale to verify (3 mg pill ÷ 3 ≈ 1 mg, close enough to target).
  • From a 5 mg tablet: roughly 1/5. Less practical to cut, but possible.
  • Mix into wet food. Ivermectin is bitter; Fancy Feast pâté (the same vehicle the existing playbook recommends for praziquantel) hides it well.
  • Don't repeat sooner than 14 days. Long half-life in cats (~3 days oral); repeated dosing accumulates.

For an adult human (stockpile reference)

Standard dose for strongyloidiasis or scabies is 200 mcg/kg, single oral dose. A 70 kg adult takes ~14 mg, which is roughly 4-5 of the 3 mg tablets, or 2 of the 6 mg, or 3 of the 5 mg.

What I'd Actually Do

recommendation ›

For Mr. Puff specifically

  1. Don't bother with oral ivermectin tablets. She doesn't need heartworm prevention if she's getting Revolution Plus (covers heartworm + fleas + ear mites + sarcoptic mange in one topical). If she develops ear mites, mineral oil cleaning + visual recheck is the first move; Acarexx is the Rx escalation.
  2. Skip the Heartgard Cats panic-buy. Even though it's discontinuing, Revolution Plus is a strict upgrade for Mr. Puff's situation.

For household stockpile

  1. If you live in an OTC state (TX, TN, AR, ID, LA): buy through a local pharmacy. Forest Park in Texas is the cleanest mail-order option for Texas residents. $69 for 40 tabs = several courses for adults.
  2. If you don't: GoodRx + 5-minute telehealth Rx (Push Health, Hims) is the cheapest legitimate route. $1.39/tab generic, made in cGMP facilities.
  3. If you want a no-Rx legitimate import option: Ivexterm via Mexipharmacy. Slightly more expensive per mg, but Valeant manufacture and US-shipping is reliable.
  4. If you want long-shelf-life USP-grade stockpile and like pill flexibility: Sacred Purity 5 mg pills, 100 ct or 1000 ct depending on appetite. Best per-mg deal at the 1000 ct.
  5. Don't buy horse paste even though it's $5/tube. Math errors on cats are fatal; on humans, dosing mistakes are merely embarrassing rather than lethal, but the format is a liability.
